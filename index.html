Governance Framework

A comprehensive taxonomy organizing governance mechanisms into pillars and evaluating voting power distribution models.

Governance Pillars

Economic Governance

Incentives, funding mechanisms, rewards distribution, and mining reserve allocation. Governs the economic sustainability and growth of the protocol.

Operational Governance

FIP process management, coordination mechanisms, multisig operations, and network upgrades. Governs day-to-day protocol operations.

Protocol Governance

Parameter changes, governance councils, voting contracts, and core protocol modifications. Governs fundamental protocol rules.

Voting Model Taxonomy

Unweighted Models

  • Pure Token Voting: 1 token = 1 vote
  • Staked Voting: Only staked tokens count
  • Identity-Based: 1 person = 1 vote with verification

Weighted Models

  • Vote Escrow (veFIL): Time-locked tokens get more weight
  • Reputation-Based: Historical contributions factor in
  • Quadratic Voting: Cost increases quadratically
  • Representative: Delegation to elected representatives

Stakeholder Pain Points

Synthesized insights from 23 interviews identifying critical friction points across key stakeholder groups in the governance lifecycle.

FIP Editors

  • High-stakes decisions without clear authority
  • Unclear role boundaries and responsibilities
  • Limited resources for proposal review
  • No formal training or succession planning
  • Burnout from volunteer workload

Implementers

  • Unclear proposal lifecycle stages
  • Low trust in governance outcomes
  • Perceived centralization concerns
  • Lack of visibility into prioritization
  • Insufficient feedback loops

Core Developers

  • No formal prioritization framework
  • Lack of credit for governance work
  • Unclear escalation mechanisms
  • Technical debt from rushed implementations
  • Limited say in roadmap direction

Storage Providers

  • Low voting participation due to complexity
  • Insufficient representation in decision-making
  • Economic impacts not fully understood pre-vote
  • Geographic barriers (APAC timezone challenges)
  • Technical knowledge gaps for proposals

Case Study: Curve Finance

Vote Escrow Success Metrics

Curve Finance pioneered the vote escrow (ve) model, demonstrating remarkable long-term alignment and participation. Key learnings inform the veFIL implementation strategy.

65% of circulating CRV locked in governance
38% average voting participation rate
4 years maximum lock duration (most common choice)
2.5x voting power multiplier for max lock
Key Takeaway: Curve's success demonstrates that vote escrow mechanisms effectively align incentives for long-term participation. Most voters voluntarily choose maximum lock periods when benefits (voting power, yield, governance influence) are properly structured. This informed Filecoin's veFIL design with similar time-weighting curves and incentive structures.
